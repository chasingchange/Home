How Much Training Volume Do You Actually Need to Build Muscle?

Most people either do too little or way too much.

Muscle growth isn’t about guessing. It’s about hitting the right number of high-quality sets per muscle each week based on your training experience.

The Chasing Change Volume Framework

Beginner (0–1 year lifting)

6–10 sets per muscle per week

Focus: learning technique, building consistency, and recovering well between sessions.

Intermediate (1–3 years lifting)

10–15 sets per muscle per week

Focus: progressive overload, balanced programming, and visible physique changes.

Advanced (3+ years lifting)

12–20 sets per muscle per week

Focus: specialization, symmetry, and targeted muscle development.

What the Research Shows

Research consistently shows muscle growth improves as weekly training volume increases, with the strongest hypertrophy results occurring around 10–20 hard sets per muscle group per week.

Primary reference:

Schoenfeld BJ, Ogborn D, Krieger JW (2017)

Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and increases in muscle mass

Journal of Sports Sciences

Supporting reference:

The Chasing Change volume model is built from modern hypertrophy meta-analyses, not guesswork programming trends.

Takeaway

If you train too little, progress stalls.

If you train too much, recovery breaks.

The goal is not maximum volume.

The goal is effective volume.

Chasing Change programs place you inside the range where growth actually happens.
