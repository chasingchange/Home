I AM

How to determine who you are, the good & the bad

I AM

Write identity statements that describe who you are today. Check the identities you want to replace directly on this same list.

Then return to each original line and add the habits and choices that made that identity true.

“I am inconsistent with sleep → stay up late scrolling, skip wind-down routine”

“I am strong → train 3x per week”

“I am anxious before social events → avoid reaching out first”

HABITS

Return to each original “I am” line and add the habits and choices that support that identity directly on that same line.

Keep the worksheet clean: do not add extra habit lines below.

I AM REDUX

Rewrite the identities you checked above. Each new identity should still begin with “I am.” Then return to the original line and name the habits, choices, and patterns that created the old identity.

Every new identity in this section must still start with “I am.”

“I am an alcoholic” → “I am a runner”

“I am inactive” → “I am someone who trains three times per week”

“I am inconsistent” → “I am someone who follows a weekly structure”

“I am always tired” → “I am someone who protects my sleep routine”

“I am bad with money” → “I am someone who tracks spending weekly”
