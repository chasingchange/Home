The Six Core System

Most programs improve one area of life. Chasing Change installs structure underneath all of them.

Body Core

The Body Core establishes visible momentum and creates the energy required to improve the rest of life.

  • a personalized weekly strength split matched to schedule and training history
  • calorie targets or structured meal frameworks depending on preference
  • a repeatable 5–10 meal rotation system for weekdays
  • a travel and disruption workout substitution matrix
  • weekly bodyweight and physique trend tracking
  • cardio integration based on fat-loss or performance goals
  • sleep anchors that support recovery and consistency
  • step-count or movement baselines when needed

Result: training stops depending on motivation and becomes part of normal weekly structure.

Mind Core

The Mind Core installs execution systems so progress continues even during stressful weeks.

  • weekly priority filtering using a 3-task execution framework
  • friction audits identifying where consistency breaks down
  • decision rules for workouts, food, and schedule conflicts
  • a weekly reset structure to prevent falling behind
  • “minimum viable week” fallback planning for busy seasons
  • consistency tracking across chosen habit targets
  • structured planning inside a quarter-based timeline system

Result: clients stop restarting routines and begin maintaining forward motion year-round.

Art Core

The Art Core restores creative output as part of identity rather than something postponed indefinitely.

  • a weekly creative block built into their calendar structure
  • one active project roadmap with milestones
  • output cadence targets (daily, weekly, or seasonal)
  • friction mapping around avoidance or perfectionism
  • identity positioning around creator vs consumer
  • accountability checkpoints tied to visible deliverables

Result: creative work becomes scheduled behavior instead of future intention.

Soul Core

The Soul Core stabilizes energy, stress, and internal clarity so progress across other cores can hold.

  • a structured weekly reflection system
  • sleep rhythm anchoring routines
  • stress reset protocols for high-pressure weeks
  • meaning-alignment check-ins during transitions
  • emotional pattern awareness across work and relationships
  • routines that protect recovery without reducing ambition

Result: clients feel steadier and clearer instead of oscillating between burnout and restart cycles.

Career Core

The Career Core evaluates whether current work supports long-term direction and lifestyle goals.

  • a 5Ps alignment audit (Pay, Place, People, Problem, Passion)
  • schedule-energy mapping across the workweek
  • role-fit evaluation against strengths and constraints
  • promotion vs pivot decision frameworks
  • transition planning timelines when needed
  • long-range positioning conversations tied to identity goals

Result: clients understand whether to optimize their role or change direction entirely.

Life Core

The Life Core installs the weekly structure that allows everything else to function consistently.

  • a weekday operating schedule template
  • weekend guardrails that protect progress
  • gym-time placement inside real calendar constraints
  • meal timing structure matched to work rhythm
  • sleep-window consistency targets
  • environment adjustments that reduce decision fatigue
  • routine stacking so habits reinforce each other

Result: progress stops feeling fragile and starts feeling automatic.

What Coaching Is

Coaching is structured guidance designed to help you move forward with clarity and consistency.

  • weekly strategy sessions to adjust direction and remove friction
  • personalized training and habit systems built around your real schedule
  • accountability that keeps momentum from disappearing between sessions
  • feedback based on your progress, not generic plans
  • decision support when routines break or life gets busy
  • long-term structure across the Body, Art, Mind, Soul, Career, and Life cores

Coaching replaces guessing with execution.

What Coaching Isn’t

Coaching is not therapy, emergency support, or passive motivation.

  • a substitute for mental health treatment
  • daily crisis intervention
  • a done-for-you transformation
  • a rigid program you must follow perfectly
  • a one-time plan without adjustment
  • a shortcut that avoids effort or consistency

Coaching works when structure meets action.
