Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Prep-level: Easy · Skillet meal · Serve with lemon wedges

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 (6- to 8-oz.) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp. dried oregano
  • 3 Tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3 cups baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 oz. Parmesan, finely grated (about 1/4 cup)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Directions

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, heat oil. Sprinkle chicken all over with salt, pepper, and oregano. Cook, turning halfway through, until browned on both sides and an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part registers 165°F, about 8 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate.
  2. In the same skillet over medium heat, melt butter. Stir in garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add tomatoes; season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until tomatoes are beginning to burst, about 5 minutes. Add spinach and cook, stirring, until spinach is beginning to wilt, 2 to 3 minutes more.
  3. Stir in cream and Parmesan and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to low and continue to simmer, stirring occasionally, until sauce is slightly reduced, about 3 minutes. Return chicken to skillet and cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through, 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Divide chicken among plates. Spoon sauce over. Serve with lemon wedges alongside.

Salmon with Spinach and Mushrooms

Prep-level: Easy · Cast iron skillet meal · High-protein dinner

Hands Free Mode: Prevent screen from sleeping (On/Off)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 6 oz fresh spinach
  • Salt and pepper, as desired
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a cast iron skillet or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. When the oil is shimmering, carefully place the salmon fillets in the pan (skin-side down if skin-on). Sear for about 4 minutes, until crispy and golden.
  3. Flip the salmon fillets and season with salt. Continue cooking for another 4 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. Remove to a plate.
  4. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the same skillet. Stir in garlic and mushrooms. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 2 minutes, until mushrooms are tender and have released liquid.
  5. Add spinach (in batches if needed), stirring as each batch wilts.
  6. Cook until spinach is fully wilted, about 1 minute. Season the spinach-mushroom mixture with salt, pepper, paprika, and garlic powder.
  7. If needed, return salmon to the skillet briefly to warm through, then serve immediately over the seasoned spinach and mushrooms.
